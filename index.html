Understanding inversion for emphasis

Inversion for emphasis happens when the normal subject–auxiliary order is reversed to make part of the sentence more prominent. It is typical of formal English, literary style, and advanced exams.

Study tools

Use these prompts to reflect on the form, the effect, and the appropriacy of inversion in your own speaking and writing.

Key takeaway

Inversion after negative and limiting expressions is grammatically marked and tends to sound formal, emphatic, or dramatic. Use it when you really want to foreground an idea.

Reflection prompt

Where in your academic writing could inversion make an argument more striking, and where would it sound too theatrical or inappropriate?
